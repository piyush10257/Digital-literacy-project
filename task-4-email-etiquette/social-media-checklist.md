 ✅ DO (Best Practices)
Privacy Check: Regularly review your privacy settings to control who can see your posts and personal information.

Think Before You Post: Ask yourself if you’d be comfortable with a future employer, professor, or parent seeing the content.

Verify Information: Check the credibility of a source before sharing news or "facts" to avoid spreading misinformation.

Network Professionally: Use platforms like LinkedIn to connect with alumni, professors, and industry professionals.

Be Respectful: Treat others with the same kindness and respect online as you would in a face-to-face conversation.

❌ DON'T (Common Pitfalls)
Overshare Private Info: Avoid posting your home address, current precise location, or phone number publicly.

Post During "Heat": Never post or reply when you are angry or upset; give yourself a "cool-down" period first.

Plagiarize Content: Don’t claim others' work, photos, or ideas as your own; always give proper credit.

Badmouth Your Institution: Avoid venting about professors or your university publicly, as this can appear unprofessional to recruiters.

Ignore Security: Don’t use the same password for all accounts or click on suspicious links sent via Direct Messages (DMs).
